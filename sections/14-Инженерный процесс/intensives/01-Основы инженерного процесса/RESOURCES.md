# Ресурсы интенсива

Это общая библиография. Внутри каждого спринта указан обязательный minimum route. Не требуется читать всё последовательно.

## Requirements and problem framing

- *Software Requirements*, Karl Wiegers and Joy Beatty, selected chapters.
- *Discovering Requirements*, Ian Alexander and Ljerka Beus-Dukic.
- *User Story Mapping*, Jeff Patton, used critically for slicing and shared understanding.
- Given/When/Then and specification-by-example materials.
- RFC 2119/8174 for normative language where formal specifications need it.

## Planning, flow and forecasting

- *Kanban*, David J. Anderson, selected flow concepts.
- *Actionable Agile Metrics for Predictability*, Daniel Vacanti.
- Little’s Law and queueing introductions.
- Monte Carlo forecasting material based on historical throughput/cycle time.
- *The Principles of Product Development Flow*, Donald Reinertsen, selected concepts.
- Critical Chain/critical-path references used comparatively, not dogmatically.

## Git and collaboration

- Pro Git and official Git documentation.
- GitHub/GitLab official documentation for pull requests, reviews, CODEOWNERS, merge queues and protected branches.
- *Working Effectively with Legacy Code*, Michael Feathers, selected change-safety concepts.
- Google Engineering Practices documentation for code review.
- *Software Engineering at Google*, selected chapters on reviews, testing, knowledge and scale.

## Quality engineering

- *Growing Object-Oriented Software, Guided by Tests*, selected general testing ideas.
- *xUnit Test Patterns*, Gerard Meszaros, reference chapters.
- *Continuous Delivery*, Jez Humble and David Farley, selected verification concepts.
- Martin Fowler articles on test strategy, test doubles and integration contracts.
- Go official testing, fuzzing, race and coverage documentation.
- staticcheck, golangci-lint and `govulncheck` official documentation.

## Technical decisions and documentation

- ADR resources by Michael Nygard and adr.github.io.
- RFC and design-document practices from mature engineering organizations.
- *Docs for Developers*, Jared Bhatti et al.
- Diátaxis documentation framework.
- C4 model and arc42 as optional communication tools.
- *The Staff Engineer’s Path*, selected decision and influence chapters.

## Teams, ownership and communication

- *Team Topologies*, Matthew Skelton and Manuel Pais.
- *Accelerate*, Nicole Forsgren, Jez Humble and Gene Kim.
- *An Elegant Puzzle*, Will Larson, selected organizational interfaces.
- *Crucial Conversations* or comparable feedback material, used selectively.
- RACI/DACI/RAPID and other decision-right models as comparative tools, not universal templates.

## Improvement and socio-technical systems

- *The DevOps Handbook*, selected flow, feedback and learning chapters.
- *Learning from Incidents in Software*, incident-learning material.
- *The Fifth Discipline* and systems-thinking introductions.
- Deming/PDSA and improvement-kata concepts.
- *Kill It with Fire*, Marianne Bellotti, for modernization and technical debt.
- SPACE framework and DORA research for measurement context.

## Rules for sources

1. Practices are hypotheses contingent on team size, risk, product and architecture.
2. Framework names do not replace explicit problems and constraints.
3. Metrics are accepted only with definitions, data quality and expected decisions.
4. A template is used only when its fields reduce uncertainty or coordination cost.
5. Case studies are not copied without comparing context and failure modes.
6. Individual productivity is never inferred from commit, ticket or review counts.
7. Official tool documentation defines version-specific behavior.
8. General mechanisms move to `knowledge`; proprietary process documents are not copied.
