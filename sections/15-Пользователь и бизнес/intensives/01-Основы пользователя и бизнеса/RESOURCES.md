# Ресурсы интенсива

Это общая библиография. Внутри каждого спринта указан обязательный minimum route. Не требуется читать всё последовательно.

## User research and service design

- *Interviewing Users*, Steve Portigal.
- *The Mom Test*, Rob Fitzpatrick, used critically and not as a complete research method.
- *Just Enough Research*, Erika Hall.
- *Observing the User Experience*, Mike Kuniavsky et al.
- Nielsen Norman Group research-method and usability articles.
- GOV.UK Service Manual and Service Design guidance.
- *This is Service Design Doing*, selected methods.
- W3C/WAI accessibility and inclusive-design material.

## Product strategy and discovery

- *Good Strategy/Bad Strategy*, Richard Rumelt.
- *Inspired* and *Empowered*, Marty Cagan, used comparatively rather than prescriptively.
- *Continuous Discovery Habits*, Teresa Torres.
- *Obviously Awesome*, April Dunford.
- *Value Proposition Design*, selected concepts.
- *Escaping the Build Trap*, Melissa Perri.
- Opportunity Solution Tree and product-bet materials used as tools, not doctrine.

## Business analysis and processes

- BABOK concepts used as reference rather than certification syllabus.
- BPMN 2.0 specification and practical modeling guidance.
- DMN and decision-table concepts.
- *Business Analysis*, Debra Paul et al., selected chapters.
- Event Storming resources for collaborative domain/process discovery.
- SIPOC, value-stream mapping and service-blueprint references used comparatively.

## Analytics and experimentation

- *Lean Analytics*, selected metric and stage concepts.
- *Trustworthy Online Controlled Experiments*, Ron Kohavi et al.
- *Experimentation Works*, Stefan Thomke.
- Causal-inference introductions covering randomization, selection bias and confounding.
- Amplitude, Mixpanel, PostHog or equivalent public analytics guides used critically.
- SQL cohort/funnel analysis references.
- A/B test statistics material from reputable university or official experimentation sources.

## Economics and business cases

- *Business Model Generation*, selected modeling concepts.
- Unit-economics and SaaS metric references from reputable finance/accounting sources.
- Discounted-cash-flow, NPV, IRR and payback introductions.
- FinOps Foundation material for cloud and technology cost allocation.
- Total cost of ownership and cost-benefit analysis references.
- Pricing research concepts: Van Westendorp, conjoint and willingness-to-pay limitations.

## Adoption, support and operations

- *Crossing the Chasm*, used for context, not universal truth.
- ADKAR and other change-management models used comparatively.
- Customer-success and service-management materials.
- Support taxonomy, knowledge-centered service and feedback-loop resources.
- Business continuity and impact-analysis guidance.

## Governance, ethics and privacy

- NIST Privacy Framework.
- GDPR and relevant privacy guidance only through current official sources when applied in real contexts.
- OECD or equivalent ethical data/AI principles where applicable.
- W3C accessibility standards and official guidance.
- Organization-specific legal/regulatory requirements must be verified with qualified counsel.

## Rules for sources

1. Research methods follow the question and participant context.
2. A framework name never substitutes for evidence or decision rationale.
3. Personas, journeys and process maps remain hypotheses until validated.
4. Metrics require definitions, data-quality checks and decision use.
5. Experiment results are bounded by population, duration and causal assumptions.
6. Financial models expose ranges, sensitivity and excluded costs.
7. Real legal, privacy, accounting and regulatory decisions require current authoritative sources and specialists.
8. No proprietary report, participant transcript or customer data is copied without authorization.
