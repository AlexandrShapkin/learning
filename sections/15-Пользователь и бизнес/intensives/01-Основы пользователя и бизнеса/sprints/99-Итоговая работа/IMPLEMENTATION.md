# Реализация итоговой работы

## 1. Reference product and synthetic organization

Create:

- Go API/web application and PostgreSQL;
- customer request, dispatch, assignment, completion and support flows;
- synthetic organizations, users, contracts, service jobs and events;
- baseline manual/offline work and intentionally flawed process;
- fixed-seed data and scenario generators.

## 2. Stakeholders and decision rights

Document:

- users, customers, buyers, sponsors, process/data owners and affected non-users;
- goals, incentives, authority, constraints and success criteria;
- conflicts and decision rights;
- outcome owner and escalation path;
- assumptions requiring research.

## 3. Research plan and evidence

Required:

- decision-linked research questions;
- method/sample/recruitment rationale;
- consent, privacy, retention and deletion plan;
- synthetic interviews for buyer, dispatcher, worker, customer and support;
- one contextual-observation workflow;
- one survey with pilot and sample limitations;
- coded evidence separating observation, quote, interpretation and insight;
- contradictory and non-adopter cases;
- evidence-strength and uncertainty assessment.

## 4. Segments, jobs and scenarios

Create:

- 3–5 observable behavioral/needs segments;
- jobs, desired outcomes, triggers, alternatives and barriers;
- problem hypotheses by segment;
- minimal evidence-based personas;
- normal, edge, accessibility/connectivity and failure scenarios;
- non-target/anti-persona constraints without stigmatization.

## 5. Journey and service blueprint

Map:

- end-to-end customer, dispatcher and worker journeys;
- goals, actions, channels, touchpoints, waits, abandonment and recovery;
- frontstage, backstage, support process, policy and system layers;
- moments of truth and evidence quality;
- ownership and handoffs;
- at least three user pains traced to operational causes.

## 6. Value, vision and positioning

Define:

- target segment and buying/use context;
- current alternatives and switching costs;
- user and buyer outcomes;
- differentiated value and evidence;
- product vision and positioning;
- explicit non-target contexts and unsupported claims.

## 7. Goals and strategy

Create:

- baseline and outcome goals;
- causal metric tree with leading, lagging and guardrail metrics;
- definitions, populations and exclusions;
- strategic diagnosis, guiding policy and coherent actions;
- 3–5 product bets with assumptions and thresholds;
- outcome roadmap with decreasing confidence;
- explicit exclusions and opportunity cost.

## 8. Prototype and validation

Use one prototype, concierge test or pilot to address the highest-risk assumption.

Required:

- uncertainty and decision;
- method and participant scope;
- ethical/operational safeguards;
- predeclared success, guardrail and stop criteria;
- manual effort and scalability evidence;
- result separating desirability, usability, feasibility and viability;
- continue, adapt or stop decision.

## 9. Business processes

Create as-is and to-be models with:

- trigger, customer, outcome and owner;
- activities, decisions, handoffs and systems;
- wait, touch time, rework and exceptions;
- manual/offline and support work;
- controls and their purpose;
- simplification/elimination before automation;
- transition, coexistence and rollback.

## 10. Business rules and decisions

Implement/document:

- eligibility, scheduling, priority, pricing and completion rules;
- decision tables/trees;
- source, owner, scope, precedence and effective dates;
- exceptions and time-bounded overrides;
- rule-version evidence in product outcomes;
- rule-change impact on process, data, requirements and analytics.

## 11. Domain events and data authority

Provide:

- shared glossary;
- roles, business events and state transitions;
- invalid/recovery transitions;
- critical data inventory;
- creator, steward and authoritative source;
- derived copy freshness and reconciliation;
- identifiers and matching logic;
- correction/history rules;
- event/effective/processing time distinctions.

## 12. Requirements and change impact

Required:

- business, stakeholder, user and system requirements;
- quality, data, reporting, failure, migration and operational requirements;
- acceptance examples and outcome links;
- solution alternatives and rejected options;
- stakeholder task, permission, workload and incentive changes;
- consultation/decision/approval boundaries;
- training, support and readiness evidence;
- unresolved dissent and transition risks.

## 13. Product instrumentation

Implement:

- event catalogue tied to decisions;
- user/account/session/workflow identity model;
- completed-fact naming and properties;
- event/effective time, source and schema version;
- duplicate/retry/order behavior;
- consent/privacy/retention limits;
- volume/null/uniqueness/order/drift checks;
- reconciliation to operational state.

## 14. Funnel and cohort analysis

Create:

- customer request, dispatcher and worker funnels;
- eligible population, order and time windows;
- activation based on first realized value;
- user, account and workflow retention definitions;
- churn, resurrection and seasonality rules;
- cohort tables/curves;
- segment and incomplete-cohort handling;
- qualitative investigation of key drop-offs.

## 15. Controlled experiment or pilot analysis

Required:

- hypothesis and decision;
- treatment/control or explicit quasi-experimental design;
- unit, assignment, exposure and contamination analysis;
- primary, secondary and guardrail outcomes;
- sample/duration/business-cycle assumptions;
- effect range and practical significance;
- peeking/multiple-comparison policy;
- segment heterogeneity labeled predeclared or exploratory;
- ship, iterate, stop or gather-more-evidence decision.

## 16. Feedback and support triangulation

Create:

- support/contact taxonomy;
- severity, frequency, affected segment and value impact;
- sensitive-data redaction;
- ticket/theme linkage to journey, process and event evidence;
- selection-bias analysis;
- silent/non-contacting user consideration;
- at least one accepted and one rejected request with rationale;
- structural routing for recurring problems.

## 17. Revenue and unit economics

Model:

- economic unit and customer/contract time horizon;
- revenue, gross margin and contribution margin;
- onboarding, support, transaction, infrastructure and partner cost;
- CAC, LTV and payback ranges;
- segment economics and cross-subsidy;
- churn, price, usage and support sensitivity;
- scalable versus manual cost.

## 18. TCO and business case

Compare:

- build;
- buy;
- process-only change;
- do nothing.

Include implementation, integration, migration, training, operation, vendor, downtime, risk and retirement. Model benefit attribution, opportunity cost, downside/base/upside scenarios, ROI/payback/NPV where appropriate and break-even drivers.

## 19. Pricing and packaging

Provide:

- candidate pricing metrics/models;
- segment packages and capability boundaries;
- value, cost, predictability and procurement analysis;
- WTP evidence with limitations;
- discounts and authority;
- adoption, usage, margin and support sensitivity;
- price-change communication and migration safeguards.

## 20. Risk and continuity

Create:

- cause-event-consequence risk register;
- business impact analysis;
- critical workflow disruption/data-loss/manual-workaround limits;
- privacy, contractual and regulatory assumptions for specialist review;
- vendor concentration, portability and exit scenarios;
- control owner, evidence and residual risk;
- continuity capacity and backlog recovery;
- one severe combined scenario.

## 21. Pilot and rollout

Implement/simulate:

- representative pilot cohort including difficult cases;
- readiness matrix for users, data, process, support and operation;
- migration, coexistence and rollback;
- role/task-based training with teach-back;
- adoption depth and workflow-value metrics;
- error, accessibility, support and workload guardrails;
- champion/resistance/shadow-process monitoring;
- pause and expansion decisions.

## 22. Customer success and product operations

Define:

- success outcomes and milestones by segment;
- time-to-value and adoption depth;
- support channels, severity and escalation;
- health model with causal assumptions and false-positive limits;
- knowledge content and validation;
- at-risk intervention boundaries;
- feedback routing to product/process ownership;
- renewal/expansion considerations without overriding harm/risk.

## 23. Portfolio decision

Assess:

- strategic fit and evidence maturity;
- user/business outcomes;
- unit economics and TCO;
- operational/support/continuity readiness;
- portfolio overlap and opportunity cost;
- scale, maintain, pivot, merge, sunset or stop options;
- sunk-cost bias;
- migration/data/support obligations;
- final decision and review threshold.

## 24. `biztriage`

Read-only commands:

```text
biztriage stakeholder
biztriage evidence
biztriage segment
biztriage journey
biztriage process
biztriage rule
biztriage requirement
biztriage metric
biztriage experiment
biztriage economics
biztriage risk
biztriage rollout
biztriage decision
```

The tool uses only local synthetic/approved artifacts, requires exact scope, exposes sources and definitions, redacts sensitive fields, limits output and never changes product, analytics, research or financial data.

## 25. Systemic review

Document:

- strongest and weakest evidence links;
- contradictions across research, process, analytics and economics;
- who benefits, pays and bears risk;
- first failure in value realization;
- product versus process versus rollout versus measurement cause;
- one falsified attractive narrative;
- no more than three prioritized next actions;
- continue, adapt, scale, sunset or stop rationale.

## 27. Implementation stages

### Stage 1. Discovery

Stakeholders, research, segments, jobs, personas, journeys and blueprint.

### Stage 2. Strategy

Value, positioning, goals, metric tree, bets, roadmap and validation.

### Stage 3. Business analysis

Processes, rules, domain data, requirements and change impact.

### Stage 4. Measurement

Instrumentation, funnels, cohorts, experiments and feedback triangulation.

### Stage 5. Economics and risk

Unit economics, TCO, pricing, business risks and continuity.

### Stage 6. Adoption

Pilot, rollout, training, support and customer success.

### Stage 7. Decision and assessment

Portfolio decision, `biztriage`, systemic review and blind cases.
