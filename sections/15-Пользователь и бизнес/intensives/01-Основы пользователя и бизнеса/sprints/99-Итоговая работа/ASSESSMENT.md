# Итоговая проверка

## Blind product/business exercise

Solve at least 26 hidden cases:

- four stakeholder/research cases;
- four segment/journey/strategy cases;
- four process/rule/requirement cases;
- four analytics/experiment cases;
- four economics/risk cases;
- four rollout/support/portfolio cases;
- two combined cross-layer cases.

For each case:

```text
Exact user, business or adoption symptom
Affected segment, role, process and decision scope
Preserved research, journey, rule, event, metric and financial evidence
Timeline of assumptions, interventions and observed outcomes
Facts, hypotheses, biases and one discriminating read-only check
First unsupported link or contradictory evidence
Minimal research, model, product or rollout correction
User outcome, business result, guardrail and risk verification
New evidence rule, metric check, experiment or decision threshold
```

## Clean reconstruction

From clean checkout and empty laboratory state:

1. verify tools, scope and synthetic-data markers;
2. build the Go/PostgreSQL reference product;
3. generate synthetic users, organizations, workflows and events;
4. map stakeholders and decision rights;
5. create research questions, sample and ethics plan;
6. analyze interviews, observation and survey evidence;
7. build segments, jobs, personas, journeys and service blueprint;
8. define value proposition, goals, strategy and roadmap;
9. run prototype/pilot validation;
10. model processes, rules, data authority and requirements;
11. instrument events and run data-quality checks;
12. calculate funnels, activation, retention and cohorts;
13. run experiment/pilot causal analysis and feedback triangulation;
14. build unit economics, TCO, pricing and risk models;
15. simulate rollout, support and adoption;
16. run `biztriage`, make portfolio decision and clean all data/services.

## Complete combined exercise

Perform one scenario:

1. a buyer requests automated scheduling and promises rapid adoption;
2. research finds dispatcher overload, worker connectivity limits and customer uncertainty;
3. a convenience survey overstates demand while non-adopters are absent;
4. the roadmap commits to a feature before resolving rule/process conflicts;
5. instrumentation duplicates assignment events and defines login as activation;
6. an account-level experiment randomizes individual users and contaminates treatment;
7. early results appear positive while support volume and worker task time worsen;
8. average unit economics look positive but a high-support segment is loss-making;
9. pilot expands to champions while shadow spreadsheets remain authoritative;
10. support capacity saturates and continuity workaround fails;
11. `biztriage` reconstructs evidence, metric, process and economic contradictions;
12. research/sample, rules, event definitions and economics are corrected;
13. rollout is paused or narrowed with new guardrails;
14. a continue, pivot, scale or stop decision is defended.

## Research and economics exercise

1. distinguish buyer, user and affected customer roles;
2. identify one leading interview question and one sampling bias;
3. rebuild an actionable segment/job and journey failure;
4. reconcile product analytics with authoritative process state;
5. expose one optimistic LTV/TCO assumption;
6. compare build, buy, process-only and do-nothing;
7. make one evidence-based pricing or stop decision.

## Completion criteria

The intensive is complete when:

- clean reconstruction creates the full synthetic product/business case;
- users, buyers, owners and affected stakeholders are distinct;
- research methods match decisions and ethical/privacy constraints;
- segments, personas and journeys are evidence-based and actionable;
- service blueprint links visible pains to backstage causes;
- value proposition and strategy contain clear choices and exclusions;
- metric tree balances user/business outcomes and guardrails;
- validation resolves a critical uncertainty and permits a stop decision;
- as-is/to-be, rules, data authority and requirements are coherent;
- event instrumentation is versioned, quality-checked and reconcilable;
- funnel/cohort conclusions survive identity, denominator and window checks;
- experiment/pilot analysis addresses contamination, peeking and practical effect;
- qualitative, support and analytics evidence are triangulated;
- unit economics and business case expose segment and sensitivity assumptions;
- pricing and risk decisions include user, cost and continuity consequences;
- rollout measures realized workflow value and can pause/rollback;
- support/customer-success loops route recurring causes to owners;
- scale/pivot/sunset/stop decision excludes sunk-cost reasoning;
- `biztriage` is read-only, scoped and redacted;
- all 56 faults are reproducible or have documented equivalent substitutes;
- at least 26 blind cases and two combined cases are solved;
- all 42 defense answers are evidence-based;
- cleanup finds no unintended identity, transcript, dataset, container or financial export;
- generalized knowledge is transferred to `knowledge`.

## Delayed assessment

After 7–14 days without rereading:

1. rebuild the laboratory from clean state;
2. answer the 42 defense questions;
3. diagnose six random faults covering research, strategy, process, analytics, economics and rollout;
4. design one new research plan and synthesize contradictory evidence;
5. build one segment/job/journey/service blueprint chain;
6. create one product strategy, metric tree and validation plan;
7. model one process/rule/data-authority conflict;
8. define events and calculate one funnel/cohort correctly;
9. design one controlled experiment with guardrails;
10. build one unit-economics/TCO sensitivity case;
11. pause, expand or stop one pilot from mixed evidence;
12. use `biztriage` without changing or exposing source data;
13. identify one metric, feature or process artifact to remove;
14. explain which remaining work belongs to UX research, product design, finance, legal, sales, marketing or organizational leadership.

После delayed assessment уровень 3–4 можно считать закреплённым.
